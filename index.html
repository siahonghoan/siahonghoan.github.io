Research Description

My work brings together phonological theory and experimental phonetics to investigate sound patterns across Sinitic languages — especially Southern Min, Hakka, and Mandarin.

One primary focus of my research is the phonetic ground of sound patterns. I use laboratory techniques (e.g., electromagnetic articulography and ultrasound tongue imaging) to study speech errors, coarticulation, and the spatiotemporal properties of speech gestures. I am also keenly interested in Austronesian languages, with a particular focus on the morphophonemics of Formosan languages.
